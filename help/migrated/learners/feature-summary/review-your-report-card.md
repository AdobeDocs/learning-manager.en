# Review your report card

Your Virtual Coach report card shows how you performed in a roleplay, including what topics you covered, how you communicated, and specific areas to improve before your next attempt.  

Every completed session automatically generates a report. You can access it immediately after a session by selecting **See Analysis**, or return to it any time from the session history in **My Learnings**.

## How your overall score is calculated

Your overall score is a weighted combination of two components: a **Knowledge score** and a **Style score**.

The author who created the scenario sets the weight of each component. A common configuration is **70% knowledge and 30% style**, but this varies by scenario.

| Component | What it measures | Typical weight |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| Knowledge | Whether you covered the required topics and provided accurate information | 70% |
| Style | How you communicated: delivery, pace, clarity, and confidence | 30% |

_The two score components and their typical weights. The exact weights for your scenario are shown on your report card._

Your overall score is displayed on a scale of **0 to 100**. The passing threshold is set by the author and shown alongside your score. If your score meets or exceeds the threshold, you pass. If not, you can retry the session.

## Knowledge score

The Knowledge score reflects **what you said** during the conversation. It measures whether you addressed the topics the author configured for the scenario.

### Topic breakdown

Each scenario has a set of required topics. Your report card shows a separate score for each topic and written feedback explaining what you covered and what you missed.  

Topics are color-coded in the session timeline at the bottom of the report, so you can see exactly when each topic was discussed during your conversation.

**Example topic scores from a discovery call scenario:**

| Topic | Score | Summary |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Opening and Rapport | 40 | Confirmed availability but did not introduce yourself by name or reference the outreach reason |
| Clinical Credibility Discovery | 60 | Identified authorship and review process but did not explicitly acknowledge the persona's accuracy concerns |
| Implementation and Resource Assessment | 40 | Described staffing and timeline but did not outline typical setup steps or confirm stakeholder involvement |
| Handling Objections and Risk | 0 | Did not invite the persona to share risks or hesitations, and did not offer documentation or mitigation steps |
| Agreeing Next Steps | 20 | Provided a project timeline but did not propose a specific low-commitment next step or secure verbal agreement |

_Topic scores show how well you addressed each required area of the conversation._

## How to improve your Knowledge score

Each topic section in your report includes specific, actionable guidance. For example:

* If you scored low on **Opening and Rapport**: greet the persona by name, state your name, briefly reference the outreach reason, and acknowledge time constraints with a short opener.  
* If you scored low on **Handling Objections and Risk**: explicitly invite the persona to share hesitations, offer to provide documentation when a concern is raised, and confirm the concern was addressed before moving on.  
* If you scored low on **Agreeing Next Steps**: propose one clear, low-commitment next step, ask to share materials by email, confirm preferred timing and attendees, and seek a verbal agreement or a clear reason for not proceeding.  

## Style score

The Style score reflects **how you communicated** during the conversation. It is based on five delivery metrics measured automatically from your audio.

### Pace

Pace measures your speaking speed in words per minute (WPM). The target range is **140–180 WPM**, which is fast enough to sound confident but slow enough to be clearly understood.

* **Below 140 WPM:** your pace was a little slow — try to speak with slightly more momentum.  
* **Above 180 WPM:** your pace was too fast — slow down to give the listener time to follow.  

Your report shows timestamps for when you spoke at your slowest and fastest, so you can replay those moments in the session recording.

### Clarity

Clarity measures how comprehensible your speech came across. A score of **90% or above** means people can understand you well. The report flags this as a pass or improvement area.

### Filler words

Filler words are sounds and phrases such as *"um", "uh", "like", "you know", and "basically"*. Using them frequently reduces your credibility and distracts from your message.

Your report shows the total count of filler words and timestamps for each instance, so you can replay and hear exactly where they occurred.

**Four or fewer filler words:** Generally acceptable for a three-minute session.  
**Five or more:** Review the flagged timestamps and practice pausing silently instead of filling the gap.  

### Sentence length

Sentence length measures whether your sentences are concise and easy to follow. The target range is 7–30 words per sentence.

* **Under 7 words:** Sentences may sound too clipped or incomplete.  
* **Over 43 words:** Sentences are likely too long and may lose the listener's attention.  

A bar indicator on the report shows where your average sentence length falls on the spectrum from too short to too long.

### Energy

Energy measures your vocal enthusiasm and confidence throughout the conversation. The report displays an energy timeline for the full session duration, color-coded from low to high.

Consistent high energy signals that you know your subject and are engaged. Dips in energy at specific moments are worth reviewing in the session recording to understand what caused them.

## Session recording and timeline

The **Session Recording** section shows your full conversation as a video playback with a synchronized timeline. The timeline displays three tracks:

* **Your speaking turns:** When you spoke during the session  
* **Persona speaking turns:** When the AI persona spoke  
* **Topics covered:** Which required topic was being addressed at each point in the conversation, color-coded to match the topic breakdown table  

Use the timeline to jump directly to specific moments. For example, the timestamp when a topic was missed or when a filler word was flagged.

## Feedback panel

If the author enabled human review, a **Feedback** appears on the right side of your report. An evaluator can leave written comments on your session here. If no evaluator has reviewed the session yet, the panel shows **"No feedback yet."**

## What to do after reviewing your report

1. Read the written feedback for each topic where you scored below your target.  
2. Note the specific improvement actions called out in each topic section.  
3. Replay the session recording at the flagged timestamps for pace, filler words, and energy.  
4. When you are ready, return to the scenario from **My Learnings** or the **Catalog** and select **Start** to begin a new attempt.  

Each new attempt is a fresh session. Your previous report remains accessible in your session history.

## Best practices

Review your lowest-scoring topic first. Improvement there will have the biggest impact on your overall score.  
Use the topic timeline to check whether you addressed required topics too late in the conversation. Raising topics earlier often improves both knowledge and style scores.  
Replay the timestamps for your slowest WPM moments. Slow pace is often caused by searching for the right word; preparing key phrases in advance can help.  
Treat a low energy score as a cue to review your opening. Energy tends to be highest at the start and dip in the middle; practice your transitions between topics to maintain momentum.
