# Virtual Coach: Overview

## What Virtual Coach does

Virtual Coach is an AI-powered role-play and assessment feature in Adobe Learning Manager that lets learners practice real-world conversations before encountering similar situations on the job.

Virtual Coach provides a practice environment where learners engage in voice or chat-based roleplays with an AI persona. The AI adapts to what the learner says in real time, simulating realistic conversations for sales calls, leadership situations, and skill assessments. After each session, learners receive an instant, detailed performance report covering what they said and how they said it.

Think of Virtual Coach as a flight simulator for communication and skills training. Just as pilots log thousands of hours in simulators before flying real aircraft, employees can practice complex conversations with an AI-driven persona before encountering similar situations with real clients, managers, or team members.

## Why traditional training falls short

Traditional corporate training approaches have several limitations that affect how well employees can apply what they learn on the job. This section explains the core challenges Virtual Coach is designed to address, and how organizations are using it in practice.

### The scalability problem

One-on-one coaching is expensive and difficult to scale. A mid-sized organization with hundreds of customer-facing employees cannot realistically provide personalized coaching to everyone. The result is that only senior or high-potential employees receive individual attention, while the majority go through generic, one-size-fits-all training that does not prepare them for the specific conversations they will face on the job.
Virtual Coach removes this constraint. A single deployment supports unlimited learners simultaneously, 24 hours a day, at a fraction of the cost of human coaching.

### The knowledge-to-skill gap

Completing a training course does not mean a learner can apply that knowledge under pressure. Traditional e-learning teaches the what, the concepts, frameworks, and facts, but rarely gives learners the opportunity to practice the how before they encounter a real situation.
A learner can pass a module on objection handling and still fumble when a CTO asks detailed questions about security architecture and compliance in a live call. Without repeated, realistic practice, knowledge stays theoretical.

### High stakes, low practice

Many critical workplace skills are first practiced in actual job situations, where mistakes have real consequences. A newly promoted manager may give performance reviews only twice a year, learning through trial and error with real employees. A new sales hire may not encounter a difficult procurement objection until their third or fourth customer call.
Virtual Coach provides learners with a consequence-free environment to rehearse these conversations as many times as needed before the real situation arises.

### Delayed and inconsistent feedback

In classroom or live coaching settings, feedback often comes days or weeks after the practice session, if it comes at all. Different instructors assess the same performance differently, making it hard for learners to understand what good looks like or track whether they are improving.
Virtual Coach provides instant, objective feedback after every session using the same evaluation criteria every time, so learners always know exactly what they did well and what to work on next.

### Accessibility and scheduling

Role-play practice in a classroom requires scheduling coordination, physical presence, and multiple participants available at the same time. For remote, distributed, or global teams across different time zones, this is often impractical.

Virtual Coach is available on any device, at any time, with no scheduling required.

### No way to measure skill improvement

Most organizations track training as course completion — a metric that shows whether someone watched a video or clicked through slides, not whether they can actually perform. Measuring improvement in skills like active listening, objection handling, or giving feedback has traditionally been too subjective and resource-intensive to do at scale.

Virtual Coach generates detailed performance data for every session, including topic coverage scores, style metrics, and improvement trends over time. Managers and L&D teams can see exactly where skill gaps exist across their teams and make evidence-based decisions about where to focus training.

## How organizations use Virtual Coach

### Sales teams: handling price objections

**The situation:** Your sales team keeps losing deals when prospects raise pricing concerns. Reps know the value proposition but struggle to articulate it under pressure in a live conversation.

**How Virtual Coach helps:** You create a scenario in which the AI plays a budget-conscious CFO who compares your product to a cheaper competitor. Sales reps practice the conversation multiple times, each time receiving personalized feedback on their negotiation approach, messaging consistency, and how confidently they linked price to business outcomes. Managers can track completion and score trends across the team to identify who needs additional coaching.

**Business impact:** Reps enter pricing conversations prepared. Messaging becomes more consistent across the team, and fewer deals are lost to cost objections alone.

### New managers: giving difficult feedback 

**The situation:** You have promoted 20 technical experts into management roles. None of them has experience delivering constructive criticism, and their first performance reviews are approaching.

**How Virtual Coach helps:** You embed a scenario in a first-time manager course in which the AI plays the role of a defensive, underperforming team member. New managers practice navigating conversations with empathy and structure, for example, using the GROW framework, and the course does not complete until they handle it successfully. They can attempt the conversation as many times as they need before their first real review.

**Business impact:** New managers arrive at their first real feedback conversation prepared rather than improvising. Employee relations improve, and the time to productivity for new leaders is reduced.

### Customer support: de-escalating angry customers

**The situation:** Your support team handles frustrated customers daily. Stress levels are high, turnover is increasing, and agents feel underprepared for emotionally charged interactions.

**How Virtual Coach helps:** You create multiple scenarios with varying customer personas
— angry, passive-aggressive, confused — so agents can practice de-escalation techniques across a range of situations. Agents can retry conversations and test different approaches in a safe environment before facing real high-emotion calls.

**Business impact:** Support agents feel more confident and handle difficult situations more calmly, improving both customer satisfaction scores and employee retention.

### Leadership development: strategic conversations

**The situation:** High-potential employees preparing for senior roles need to practice strategic thinking and executive-level communication before they assume those roles.

**How Virtual Coach helps:** Scenarios such as presenting a risky innovation strategy to a skeptical board member give emerging leaders a space to practice articulating vision, handling resistance, and building consensus. The AI adapts its responses based on how well the leader addresses concerns, and detailed feedback on executive presence and strategic communication helps them improve with each attempt.

**Business impact:** The leadership pipeline arrives at senior roles better prepared, reducing the ramp time and risk associated with high-stakes promotions.

## User personas

Virtual Coach serves three roles in Adobe Learning Manager:

* **Authors** design and publish roleplay scenarios using templates or an AI assistant.
* **Learners** complete roleplays to build skills and receive instant feedback.
* **Administrators** track usage, manage credits, and view performance reports.

## What learners practice

Virtual Coach supports three domains:

**Sales enablement:** Learners practice discovery calls, cold calls, objection handling, and negotiation. For example, a sales rep can practice handling pricing objections with a simulated budget-conscious CFO before their first real customer conversation.

**Leadership development:** New and experienced managers practice giving feedback, navigating performance conversations, and handling strategic discussions. A newly promoted manager can rehearse a difficult feedback conversation multiple times before their first real review.

**Skills assessment:** Organizations use Virtual Coach to verify that learners can apply knowledge in realistic situations, moving beyond course completion as the only measure of readiness.

## How scoring works

Each roleplay session produces two types of scores:

* **Knowledge score:** Whether the learner covered the required topics and conveyed accurate information
* **Style score:** How the learner communicated, including pace, clarity, filler words, sentence strength, and vocal energy
* **Overall score:** How the learner has performed from an overall perspective covering all the factors mentioned above.

Authors set the weight for each score when configuring the scenario. A typical configuration is 70% knowledge and 30% style.

## Key capabilities

* **Voice-based interaction:** Learners speak naturally with an AI persona represented by an avatar.
* **Real-time adaptation:** The AI responds based on what the learner says, requiring them to think on their feet.
* **Instant feedback:** Post-session reports include metrics on pace, clarity, energy, topic coverage, and more.
* **Scalable practice:** Thousands of learners can practice simultaneously without the need for human instructors.
* **Multilingual support:** Available in 16 languages (including German-Germany, Spanish-LATAM, Spanish-Spain, French-France, Italian-Italy, Portuguese-Portugal, Portuguese-Brazil, Netherlands-Dutch, and English) for global organizations.
* **Multiple deployment options:** Works as standalone job aids, embedded course modules.

## How Virtual Coach is licensed

Virtual Coach is available as an add-on subscription to Adobe Learning Manager. Usage is measured in Monthly Active Users (MAUs). An MAU credit is consumed the first time a learner completes a session in a calendar month, regardless of how many sessions they complete that month.
